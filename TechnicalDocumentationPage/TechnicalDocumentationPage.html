Introduction

In this website we will learn about video editing. To begin we will learn what is video editing. We will then move on to learn what the goals of editing are and what methods, processes and softwares are used to perform this task.

"Video editing is the process of manipulating and rearranging video shots to create a new work. Editing is usually considered part of the post production process" which also includes sound design, film scoring and sound mixing.

Goals of Editing

In this section you will learn the goals of editing.

  • Remove unwanted footage - This is one of the simplest tasks in the process and the most basic.

  • Choose the best footage - Here is where you need to use your judgement to select the best shots that serve the story you want to tell.

  • Create a flow - With all the footage you have selected you have to put all together in the order that best serves the story. You have to make sure it makes sense to you and your audience.

  • Add effects, graphics, music, etc. - Add effects to the footage you have put together. Make sure it makes sense to the scene and that it adds to the story. If doing an informational video make sure to add graphics to support the information you are sharing. Finally add music to create a musical connection between the characters and your audience.

Methods

There are different types of video editing. Here are some of the major types of editing.

  • Film Splicing - This is one of the oldest forms of editing. "Traditionally, film is edited by cutting sections of the film and rearranging or discarding them. The process is very straightforward and mechanical."

  • Tape to Tape (Linear) - This is the "original method of editing electronic video tapes before editing computers became available in the 1990s." Today this is not the desired method but in some situations it is still used.

  • Digital/Computer (Non-Linear) - This is the most modern way of editing today. In this method, footage is recorded through a video camera and then the footage is transferred to a hard drive where the editor then grabs that footage and imports it into an editing software and begins the editing process.

Software

There is a large variety of editing softwares out there but here are the major ones.

  • Hitfilm Express - A free open source software that is good for editing video and audio in addition to doing effects and compositing.

  • DaVinci Resolve - A free non-commercial and industry standard software that is good for editing video and audio in addition to doing color correction.

  • iMovie - A non-commercial macOS video editing software.

  • Adobe Creative Cloud - Includes Premiere Pro, Adobe Audition, Adobe After Effects and Media Encoder. This is a commercial industry standard video editing, audio editing and Effects creation software package that you can get with a license that suits your needs.

  • Avid Media Composer - An industry standard video editing software where you can purchase a license that suits your needs.
